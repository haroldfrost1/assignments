2. International Codes of Ethics

2.1 Code evaluation on the case study and stakeholders

In this report, Code of Conduct from two groups have been reviewed, which are Australian Computer Society (ACS) and British Computer Society (BCS). Both Codes have listed the primacy of public interest as their first item. It can be well used to evaluate this case study. In regards to these two Code, ACS suggests "the public interest takes precedence over personal, private and sectional interests, and any conflicts should be resolved in favour of the public interest", while BCS also claims "have due regard for public health, privacy, security and wellbeing of others and the environment". In this case study, national safety shall be considered as public interest, which should be taken precedence over personal privacy, as national safety could benefit the whole community while protecting personal privacy focuses on individuals. Hence, the Abbott Government is most likely to be supported by the Code, as the Abbott Government's goal is to enhance national security, although in the way of "sacrificing" privacy which has a lower precedence regarding to the Code.

2.2 Difference between Codes

Although both Codes have similar suggestions in general, the Code from ACS seems to be more generously addressing ethics for anyone in the IT industry, while BCS has specific items for specific group. BCS has an item focused on "Duty to Relevant Authority", which suggests "NOT disclose or authorise to be disclosed, or use for personal gain or to benefit a third party, confidential information except with the permission of your Relevant Authority, or as required by Legislation". In relation to this case study, the Abbott Government, which is the relevant authority, has taken effort to follow this code, so they try to pass the act of data retention through a lawful way. However, this item is absent the Code from ACS.
